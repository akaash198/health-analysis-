HEART ATTACK

A heart attack occurs when the flow of blood to the heart is blocked.
Common heart attack signs and symptoms include:

    Nausea
    Sudden dizziness
    Heart Burn
    Shortness of breathe
    Fatigue

ARRHYTHMIA

An arrhythmia is a disorder of the heart that affects the rate or rhythm at which the heart beats.
Common arrhythmia signs and symptoms include:

    Heartbeat too fast (tachycardia)
    Heartbeat too slow (bradycardia)
    Heartbeat too early (premature contraction)
    Heartbeat too erratically (fibrillation)

    CONGENTIAL HEART DEFECT

    Congenital heart disease, or a congenital heart defect, is a heart abnormality present at birth.
    Common congential heart defect signs and symptoms include:

      breathlessness or trouble breathing
      chest pain
      bluish lips, skin, fingers, and toes
      delayed growth
      abnormal heart rhythms

    BLOOD CLOT

    A blood clot is a clump of blood that has changed from a liquid to a gel-like or semisolid state.
    Common blood clot signs and symptoms include:

      swelling
      pain
      tenderness
      a warm sensation
      reddish discoloration
